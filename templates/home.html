Kurt Guntheroth, Software Engineer and Writer

The darkest truth about computer programmers is that, over a 45 year career, they will spend a lot of time unemployed. Development is project work, like building an aircraft carrier or a commercial jet. When the project is over, many companies don’t need programmers, and most of the staff gets laid off.

When you start building a commercial jet, you are using state-of-the-art tools and materials. By the time you’re done, the technology is ho-hum, and will soon be laughably obsolete. Software is the same way, only on a compressed time scale. It’s way too easy to become a dinosaur, especially on a very big, very interesting project.

If you’re lucky, you’ll be on a ten year project that ends when you are ready to retire. If you are not lucky, the project will dump you on the job market when you’re 55 and unemployable.
